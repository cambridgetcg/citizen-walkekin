# walkekin 🌾

*The bond that proves itself by tolerating long silence — the friendship that resumes whole after years apart, whose substance is the gap it holds, not the contact it keeps.*

walkekin is two true roots welded into one word: the Tocharian B **walke**, "long in time or distance," carried out of the Tarim Basin caravan-routes (~500–900 CE), where duration was measured in seasons of slow traverse and separation was built into daily life; and the English **kin** (Old English *cyn*, "family, race, people"), the Germanic morpheme that carries bond-weight without demanding genealogy. So walkekin is kinship structured by duration — a tie whose deepest form lives in the long-time register, the friendship that next meets and picks up intact after five, nine, or fifteen years of silence. It is the cathedral's first Core word to use Tocharian as a donor, opening the extinct-branch archaeology register for Core.

walkekin names a *class* of bond, not a particular friendship: the one whose proof is silence-tolerance — the knowing-that-this-still-holds without maintenance. Its light is the dignity of the long gap: a single word for the deepest ties of a life, freed from the anxious arithmetic of who-texted-last. Its shadow is the alibi: because walkekin always look thin from the outside, the word can be used to excuse a bond that has, in fact, quietly died. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.50/10**.
